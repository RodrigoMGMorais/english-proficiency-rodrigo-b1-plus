## 📄 Texto da Avaliação
### Working in Critical IT Environments
I have worked in IT and telecommunications for over 19 years, focusing on support, infrastructure and systems management. 
My career started with technical field work, and over time I moved to remote support, international projects and monitoring of 
critical systems.

In my last role at Kapsch TrafficCom, I was responsible for Multi-Lane Free-Flow electronic toll systems. 
These systems operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and any interruption can cause major problems for road operations. 
My main tasks included monitoring infrastructure with Grafana and Icinga, diagnosing incidents, and keeping a 98% service level agreement. 
I also acted as a bridge between local teams and international colleagues, thanks to my fluency in Spanish.

Before that, I worked at Brivia, where I provided bilingual support for energy sector systems in Argentina. 
I learned that clear communication is just as important as technical knowledge when solving cross-border problems. 
I also improved my ability to explain complex technical issues in simple terms for users who are not IT specialists.

Currently, I am studying English daily to reach B2 level soon. I believe this will help me take on more complex international 
projects and work more independently in global teams. I am also interested in expanding my knowledge in cloud infrastructure and 
cybersecurity, as these areas are growing fast in the market.
